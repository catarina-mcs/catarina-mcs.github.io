Learn a little More about me

Growing up, deciding on a career path was a big challenge. There were so many possibilities and my interests were so diverse, it was hard to pick a single subject and commit to it. In the end, I decided on marketing and went on to pursue both a Bachelor’s and a Master’s degree. I was lucky enough to find work in the industry, work which allowed me to explore and learn about all the different sides of marketing. Slowly, I began to realize that, as much as I loved studying the topic, I didn’t find the actual work as fulfilling and exciting. Around that time, I was tasked with helping to restructure and redesign the website of the company I worked for and during that process, I kept thinking “there has to be a better way to do this than just following our gut”. And that is how I discovered UX/UI design and how my life started to change.

A few months later, when our world was turned upside down due to a pandemic and my professional situation shifted, I decided to take the bad with the good and give UX/UI design a real try. I found the Interaction Design Foundation, did several of their courses and worked on a few personal projects. I got really excited about this new path and so, when my big brother suggested “if you’re learning how to design websites, why don’t you learn how to code them as well?”, I finally followed his advice. I never, in a million years, thought that I would find web development interesting or even be remotely good at it. I entered this world with no expectations and maybe that is what made falling in love with it even more exciting. All of a sudden, I found myself completely immersed in courses and tutorials, wanting to learn a million different things and coding nonstop for hours. I loved the creativity and problem-solving involved in writing the code. How objective failure and sucess are - either the code does what you intended it to or it doesn’t. The magic of seeing beautiful designs come to life as you type line after line. The wonderful feeling of finding that one typo that drove you crazy for hours. I loved it all. For the first time, I finally felt like I had found my thing.

Now, after a long and inspiring learning period, I’m ready to continue the journey while being a part of something bigger. I can’t wait to see where this road will take me, but I’ll make sure to continue enjoying every step of it.
